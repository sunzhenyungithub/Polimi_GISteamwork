Result

Error Matrix

The initial overall accuracy of the classification method trained with R, evaluated using a test set of 300 points, is 0.7667, as depicted in the error matrix.

The error matrix shows that our landslide susceptibility measure have a relatively reliable result. But the result are affected by many factors including the threshold of small pitch size(50, 100 or 200 pixels) the total number of our samples and ratio of training dataset and test dataset. For higher accuracy, repeating the lab is necessary.

Population per Susceptibility class

The analysis showed that the total area at significant risk of landslides cannot be ignored. There is a small proportion(22%) of population living the safe region, but a considerable portion(54.8%) of population resides areas that are vulnerable to an very high risk of landslide. Even some areas have hillslide probability that we filter them at the NLS computation during the lab.
